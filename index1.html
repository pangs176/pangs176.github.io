BORSCHT

Borscht also called borsch, borsht, or bortsch, beet soup of the Slavic countries. Although borscht is important in Russian and Polish cuisines, Ukraine is frequently cited as its place of origin.

INGREDIENTS

Beetroot peeled 300g

Small onion 1

Small carrot 1

Stick of celery 1

Small leek 1

Medium floury potatoes eg Maris Piper, peeled and cut into small dice

Ingredients above are all peeled and cut into small dice or rings


Small cabbage, shredded ½

Grains all spice 2

Bay leaf ½

Gelatinous beef stock 1.5l

Cloves garlic, peeled and crushed 4

Cider vinegar 2 tbsp


Butter 50g

Sugar 1 tsp

Ground black pepper ½tsp

Sour cream and fresh dill to serve




STEPS

1. Cut ¾ of the peeled beetroot into small dice (you may want to wear rubber gloves to do this) and set the rest aside.

2. Melt the butter in a large pan, and then soften the onion over a gentle heat for 5 minutes.

3. Add the carrot, leek, celery, diced beetroot, all spice and bay leaf and stir well to coat with butter.

4. Cook for another 10 minutes, adding a little stock if the vegetables begin to look dry. Meanwhile, grate the remaining beetroot.

5. Pour in the rest of the stock and the potatoes and simmer for 15 minutes, then add the cabbage, garlic and grated beetroot. Cook until all the vegetables are tender (about 10 minutes).

6. Add the vinegar, sugar, pepper and a pinch of salt to your tast. Add a little more of any of these if necessary


Now serve with a dollop of sour cream, a spring of dill, and some Polish bread on the side.
